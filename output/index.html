Decorating PHP classes without a common ancestor

By Ryan on Tuesday 15 March 2011 (Comments and Reactions)

The decorator--or wrapper--pattern is most often used to extend the functionality of the public interface of a specific instance of a class at runtime (rather than at definition-time). Because the behavior is changed at runtime, it allows the functionality of objects instantiated from classes in other libraries, even ones to whose source you may not have access, to be modified. Most commonly, the decorator pattern is described as a pair of classes--a component and a decorator--which derive from a common ancestor. [more]
